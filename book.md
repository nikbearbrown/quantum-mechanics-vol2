# Quantum Mechanics Vol. 2: Formalism and the Three-Dimensional Atom

**Author:** Nik Bear Brown

---

## One-Sentence Pitch
The simulation-first undergraduate volume that teaches the student to work in Dirac notation, manipulate angular momentum and spin, and solve any central-force problem — culminating in hydrogen and the periodic table.

## The Argument
Quantum mechanics is hard because its objects have no classical analogue and the standard textbook gives no way to build intuition for them. This volume pairs every concept with an LLM-generated, student-modifiable D3 simulation, so the student sees each new object before manipulating its algebra. It is Volume 2 of the *Quantum Mechanics* series.

## The Gap
Griffiths/Townsend are rigorous but static; video and applets are visual but non-rigorous and unmodifiable. This volume gives the student a third object: a simulation they built, governed by physics they can derive, that they can change and watch respond.

## The Reader
Second undergraduate QM course / first half of junior-year QM. Enters with the prerequisites carried from the prior volume (or, for Volume 1, ODEs, complex numbers, and introductory mechanics/waves). Wants to *do* quantum mechanics, not just read about it. The optional **Quantum Mechanics Vol. 5 — Math for Quantum Physics** companion supplies any math refresher, indexed to the chapter the student is on.

## High-Level Outline
- Act One (1–4): the abstract state — Hilbert space, observables, commutators, dynamics.
- Act Two (5–8): angular momentum and spin, including addition.
- Act Three (9–11): hydrogen, identical particles, and the built atom.
