# FACTCHECK — vox-3d-4s-paradox

Source: `quantum-mechanics-vol2/chapters/11-capstone-the-atom.md` (§ "The 3d/4s Subtlety")

## Claims verified

**B02 — Neutral iron configuration**
"Iron fills: argon core, then 3d six, then 4s two."
Source verbatim: "Iron's neutral ground state is [Ar] 3d⁶ 4s²." ✓
NIST confirms: Fe ground level is 3d⁶4s² ⁵D₄. ✓

**B03 — Fe²⁺ configuration**
"The doubly-charged ion is argon core, 3d six — no 4s at all."
Source verbatim: "The ion Fe²⁺ is [Ar] 3d⁶ — not [Ar] 3d⁴ 4s². When iron is doubly ionized, both 4s electrons are removed before any 3d electrons." ✓
NIST confirms: Fe²⁺ ground configuration is 3d⁶ ⁵D. ✓

**B06 — Orbital energies depend on occupation**
"Each electron moves in the average field of all the others. When you change the number of electrons, you change the effective potential every electron feels."
Source verbatim: "orbital energies in multi-electron atoms are not fixed properties of the orbital label. They depend on how many electrons are in every other orbital, because each electron contributes to the effective potential felt by all the others." ✓

**B07 — Madelung rule applies only to neutral atoms**
"The Madelung rule reflects this — it is a rule for neutral ground-state atoms."
Source verbatim: "The Madelung filling order applies to neutral ground-state atoms; the ionization removal order follows the orbital energies in the ion, which may differ." ✓

**B08 — 3d drops below 4s in the ion**
"Remove two electrons. The effective potential changes. 3d sinks relative to 4s in the ion."
Source verbatim: "Remove two electrons, and the 3d energy drops relative to 4s — the effective potential changes." ✓

**B09 — Filling order ≠ removal order**
"Filling uses the neutral atom energies. Removal uses the ion energies."
Source: "The Madelung filling order applies to neutral ground-state atoms; the ionization removal order follows the orbital energies in the ion, which may differ." ✓
