# About Quantum Mechanics: Formalism and the Three-Dimensional Atom

GitHub repository: [quantum-mechanics-vol2](https://github.com/nikbearbrown/quantum-mechanics-vol2)

Author: Nik Bear Brown

## Book Summary

`quantum-mechanics-vol2` is organized as a chapter-based book project. The chapters below outline the book's main instructional arc and source files.

## Chapter Summary

1. **Chapter 1 — The Formalism: Hilbert Space, Dirac Notation, and Operators** (`chapters/01-the-formalism.md`) - The wave function $\psi(x)$ gives the probability amplitude for finding a particle at position $x$.
2. **Chapter 2 — Observables, Hermiticity, and the Spectral Theorem** (`chapters/02-observables-and-the-spectral-theorem.md`) - *How the requirement that measurements return real numbers forces the entire structure of quantum observables.* The momentum operator is $\hat{p} = -i\hbar\,\partial_x$.
3. **Chapter 3 — Commutators, Compatibility, and the Generalized Uncertainty Principle** (`chapters/03-commutators-and-uncertainty.md`) - When two observables are measured on a large ensemble of identically prepared systems, the spread in their outcomes is not simply a technical limitation — it is a property of the quantum state itself.
4. **Chapter 4 — Quantum Dynamics: Time Evolution and the Pictures** (`chapters/04-quantum-dynamics-and-the-pictures.md`) - Quantum mechanics can describe time evolution in more than one way.
5. **Chapter 5 — Quantum Mechanics in Three Dimensions** (`chapters/05-quantum-mechanics-in-three-dimensions.md`) - The central result is a separation of variables that splits the problem into a universal angular equation — solved once for all central potentials — and a one-dimensional radial equation whose form depends on the specific potential.
6. **Chapter 6 — Angular Momentum** (`chapters/06-angular-momentum.md`) - *How three commutation relations determine an entire spectrum, without solving a single differential equation.* This chapter derives the complete spectrum of angular momentum from algebra alone.
7. **Chapter 7 — Spin and the Bloch Sphere** (`chapters/07-spin-and-the-bloch-sphere.md`) - In February 1922, Otto Stern and Walther Gerlach sent a beam of neutral silver atoms through an inhomogeneous magnetic field and collected them on a glass plate.
8. **Chapter 8 — Addition of Angular Momenta** (`chapters/08-addition-of-angular-momenta.md`) - *Why the hydrogen atom's most famous spectral line comes down to a single minus sign.* The hydrogen 21-centimeter line is the most observed spectral line in radio astronomy.
9. **Chapter 9 — The Hydrogen Atom** (`chapters/09-the-hydrogen-atom.md`) - *One formula Balmer had no explanation for.
10. **Chapter 10 — Identical Particles** (`chapters/10-identical-particles.md`) - Spectroscopists in the early 1920s catalogued the helium spectrum in fine detail.
11. **Chapter 11 — Capstone: The Atom, Built from Simulations** (`chapters/11-capstone-the-atom.md`) - *How a spectroscopist in 1927 knew the ground state of iron before anyone could explain why.* The spectroscopist's notebook for iron, 1927.
